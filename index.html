About Me

I am a PhD student in the College of Engineering at Northeastern University. I am a member of MAGICS Lab (Multi AGent Intelligent Complex Systems, and my advisor is Babak Heydari.

My research interests is at the intersection of applied artificial intelligence and social science. Currently, my focus is on analyzing the causaf effect of system-level treatments on users' interaction patterns in digital platform: who is more affected by ChatGPT? what type of users? and why is that?

Research Interests

My research focuses on the intersection of AI/Statistics and social science. I am particularly interested in:

  • Modeling/Forecasting Users Behaviors or Adverse System Outcomes: Applying Generative AI (Transformers, CNNs, RNNs, LSTM, GRU, LLMs), Classic ML (XGBoost, KNN, naive bayes, regression/...);
  • Estimating the Causal Effect of System-Level Treatments: Using causal inference (regression discontinuity, DiD, event study, synthtic control model.
